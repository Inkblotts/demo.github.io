Failure is not equal to end of life


The education system is so very syllabus-oriented that it hardly attempts to extract the best from each and every student. There is no scope of recovering once a student lags behind in the mainstream. Parents fear this the most. As a result they pressurize their children, constantly warn them of the consequences of failure and in some cases, even threaten them if they barely show signs of coming off the track. Why can’t we all be little more mature, a little more responsible for ourselves, our society? First of all, if we students are responsible enough to manage our academics sincerely the question of failing and its consequences doesn’t even arise! Our parents do not want to gain anything from our success; they just want to see us come up with something worthy in our life. Parents, on the other hand, should realize the fact that their children’s life is more valuable than their success. Talk to them, share their problems regarding academic and personal issues, tell them about their priorities, and don’t impose upon them yours. Success and failure are just two faces of the same coin! Don’t get so desperate to win that you lose your mind if you fail. Always remember: “Failure is just a momentary loss; suicide proves that you are a permanent loser!”
